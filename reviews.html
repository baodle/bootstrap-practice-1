Chips Reviews

Calbee Seaweed Potato Chips

Potato chips with seaweed flavors. The taste can vary on whether you like seaweed or not. But if you do, this can be one of the best chips you have ever eaten. The salty and savory flavor of seaweed mixes together with the crunchiness of potato chips to create a uniquely satisfying and addictive snack experience. Can be found in your local Asian supermarkets.

Chester's Fries

The sister brand of Cheetos. Offer a slightly lower degree of spicyness with an airier crunchiness when compared to the original Cheetos. As the name suggest, this chip does give a hint of french fries when bite into it. If you are craving a spicy snack but don't want to go overboard, this is definitely the one to go with.

Classic Lays

Good old classic potato chips flavor. You can never go wrong with this. Definitely can be described as the most consistent chip in the market. Although the taste can be bland after a while.

Flamin' Hot Lays

One of the most popular flavors of Lays. A must pick for the ones that like spicy foods. The spicyness help elevates the taste of potato chips to a new level. Although the recipe has been modified several times over the years, the taste is still there and it continues to evoke memories of comfort and satisfaction with every bite.

Flamin' Hot Cheetos

One of the oldest chips out there yet still maintaining its popularity throughout the years. The spicyness along with its crunchiness work together to attack your taste buds, making you want to continue eating it nonstop. If you are a fan of spicy chips, you should really give this a try.

Funyuns Flamin' Hot Onion Rings

Spicy version of Funyun's onion rings. With each crispy bite, you're greeted by a burst of intense heat that perfectly complements the good old savory onion flavor. The spicy kick increase the flavor of the familiar taste, creating an unforgettable snacking experience.

Funyuns Onion Rings

Crunchy corn-based chips with a hint of onion flavor on top of it. The crispy crunch of the chips perfectly complements the subtle yet savory notes of onion, creating a satisfying snacking experience. Not something I would like to eat often, but definitely a perfect treat every once in a while.

Jalapenos Cheetos

Basically the non-spicy version of Cheetos with japapenos flakes on top of it. The mild heat of the jalapenos complements the cheesy taste of the Cheetos, adding a subtle kick without overpowering the overall flavor profile. Definitely give this a try at least once.

Sour Cream & Onion Lays

Offer a tangy and savory flavor with a hint of sourness from the onions. Definitely more flavorful when compared to the regular lays. There's no reason not to try this if you come across it. Though throughout my experience, I have met a lot of people who dislikes onion so this review might not apply to those.

XXXTra Flamin' Hot Cheetos

One of the newer flavors of Cheetos in recent years. It's basically hot cheetos but about 2-3 hotter. If you find the spicyness of regular hot Cheetos somewhat tame after a while, then this is a must try.
(P/S: The aftermath can be severe so be careful)
